About

FROM MAPS TO MECHANISMS

My focus shifted from developing geospatial solutions to pursuing my passion of leveraging game theory to facilitate coordination in decentralized systems. Previously at Apple Maps, I managed pilot projects integrating pedestrian experiences and AR technology. However, my background in economics and environmental studies always kept me fascinated by game theory and how incentives shape behavior.

Discovering Tim Roughgarden's cryptoeconomics course was a turning point – it revealed how blockchain could architect entirely new coordination mechanisms. Now, about a year into this career shift, I focus on building and learning about frameworks that leverage aligned incentives to solve coordination failures, creating systems where collaboration naturally yields positive-sum outcomes.

Projects

MerkleProofVoting

Governance & Voting

A secure, efficient blockchain-based voting system utilizing cryptographic Merkle trees to enable transparent decision-making for DAOs and communities.

  • Customizable Proposal Creation
  • Gasless & Cryptographic Voter Authentication
  • Tamper-Proof Ballot Security
  • Transparent Decision Execution

Viridian

Environmental Impact

A protocol leveraging bonding curves and prediction markets to quantify and tokenize ecological service impact with the goal to incentivize more global participation in environmental impact projects.

  • Market-Driven Conservation Incentives
  • Decentralized Impact Verification
  • Evidence-Based Ecological Value Pricing
  • Performance-Based Retroactive Rewards

Proof of Change

Climate Finance

A decentralized GIS platform that verifies and validates real-world environmental impact through SUAVE confidential compute, satellite imagery analysis, and blockchain attestations, powered by Astral Protocol's Logbook and Location Proof Protocol.

  • Retroactive Funding for Verified Results
  • Outcome-Based Incentive Mechanisms
  • Democratized Access as a Public Good
  • Transparent Location-Based Contribution Ecosystem

KyeDAO

Community Finance

A decentralized protocol that modernizes the traditional Korean "Kye" system - a rotating savings and credit association (ROSCA) commonly used in immigrant communities - making community-based lending more secure, transparent, and accessible.

  • Community-Governed Lending Cycles
  • Automated Rotating Credit Distribution
  • Transparent On-Chain Transaction History
  • Elimination of Single Points of Failure
